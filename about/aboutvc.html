The Vatican City... a Country?

Many people often believe that their hometowns are rather small and quaint, do to this... how would they feel about knowing an entire country is smaller in both size and population?

     Located in Rome, Italy along the west bank of the Tiber River, with a population of around 800-900 clergy and Swiss soldiers,Vatican City sleeps as the forefront of Roman Catholicism and a symbol of Rome that still persists to the modern day.

     Vatican City has no income tax nor any restrictions on monetary imports and exports making the nation even more of a unique country than it already is.

     The walled city has a surprising degree of buildings apart from holy sites. Among the Vatican’s repertoire lies a post office, telephone system, astronomical observatory, a pharmacy, banking system AND a radio station. This Roman city-state is built to be a functional town AND country!

     Among the Vatican’s many buildings, none may stand out more than the museums, or Musei Vaticani. These museums, however, are not the only picture-worthy architecture in the city. Such notoriously beautiful structures include the “Palazzo del Governatorato” (Governor’s Palace) and the Pope’s residence; the “Apostolic Palace”.
