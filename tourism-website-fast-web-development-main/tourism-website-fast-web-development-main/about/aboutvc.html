The Vatican City... a Country?

Many people often believe that their hometowns are rather small and quaint. Due to this... how would they feel about knowing an entire country could be smaller in both size and population?

     Located in Rome, Italy along the west bank of the Tiber River, with a population of around 800-900 clergy and Swiss soldiers and a size of roughly 0.17 square miles; Vatican City sleeps as the forefront of Roman Catholicism and remains a symbol of ancient Rome that still persists to the modern day. The people may change through the ages, their clothes and actions may fade into the great ocean of history, but their symbols still remain; allowing the generations who have come and who are to come experience another, different world. The following are some curious traits belonging to this time-forged micronation.

     Vatican City has a particularly rare trait- according to an article published under NYU LAW, The holy city no income tax nor any restrictions on monetary imports and exports, and beyond that the country even has it's own secretive bank!

     The walled city has a surprising degree of buildings apart from holy sites. According to an article by Britannica, among the Vatican's repertoire lies a post office, telephone system, astronomical observatory, a pharmacy, banking system AND a radio station. This Roman city-state is built to be a functional town AND country!

     Among the Vatican's many buildings, none may stand out more than the museums, or Musei Vaticani. These museums, however, are not the only picture-worthy architecture in the city. Such notoriously beautiful structures as detailed in building-data oriented website Emporis include the "Palazzo del Governatorato"(Governor's Palace) and the Pope's residence, the "Apostolic Palace".
